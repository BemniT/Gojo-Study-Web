# 1. School Admin Portal

> **Who uses this?** School administrators who manage the day-to-day operations of the school — students, teachers, parents, schedules, and communication.

---

## Logging In

1. Open the Admin web app in your browser.
2. You will see a **Login** page.
3. Enter your **username** and **password**.
4. Click **Login**.
5. If your account has the correct admin role, you will be taken to the **Dashboard**.

> If you see an error, double-check your username and password. Your username was created by the system when your account was set up.

---

## The Layout

After logging in you will see three areas on the screen:

- **Left sidebar** — the main navigation menu. Click any item to go to that section.
- **Center** — the main content area where the page loads.
- **Top bar** — shows your profile picture, a notification bell (for new posts), and a message icon (for unread messages). Click the bell or message icon to see what is new.
- **Right panel** — appears when you click on a teacher, student, or parent to show their details.

---

## Dashboard

The Dashboard is your home page. It shows the school's activity feed.

**What you can do here:**
- Read announcements and posts that have been published to the school.
- **Create a new post** — click the **Create Post** button, type your message, and choose who should see it (Teachers, Students, Parents, or Everyone). You can also attach a photo or video.
- Edit or delete posts you have created.
- See how many unread messages you have from each person (shown as a badge on the message icon).

---

## Overview (Analytics)

The Overview page shows a summary of the school's enrollment numbers.

**What you will see:**
- **Total students** — how many students are currently enrolled.
- **Active vs Inactive** — how many students have active accounts.
- **Gender breakdown** — male and female percentages.
- **New registrations this month** — how many students joined compared to last month, shown as a trend.
- **Students by grade** — a bar chart and percentage bars showing how many students are in each grade.

---

## Managing Teachers

Go to **Teachers** in the left sidebar.

**The teacher list:**
- All your school's teachers are shown in a scrollable list.
- You can **filter by grade** (to see which teacher teaches that grade) or **search by name or subject**.

**Clicking on a teacher opens a detail panel on the right with three tabs:**

### Details Tab
Shows the teacher's full profile: name, email, phone number, assigned subjects, and whether their account is active or inactive.

### Schedule Tab
Shows the teacher's complete weekly timetable — which class they teach, on which day, and during which period.

### Lesson Plans Tab
Shows the lesson plans that teacher has submitted through the Teacher Portal. You can browse week by week.

**Actions you can take:**
- **Activate / Deactivate** a teacher account — you will be asked to re-enter your admin password before this takes effect, as a safety check.
- **Chat** with a teacher — a chat popup opens on the right side. You can send and receive messages without leaving the Teachers page.
- **Export timetable as PDF** — downloads the teacher's schedule as a PDF file.

---

## Managing Students

Go to **Students** in the left sidebar.

**The student list:**
- All students are shown in a scrollable, searchable list.
- Filter by **grade**, **section**, or type a name to search.

**Clicking on a student opens a full detail panel on the right with multiple tabs:**

| Tab | What you see |
|-----|-------------|
| Basic Info | Name, grade, section, gender, date of birth, profile photo |
| Parent Info | Linked parent names and their relationship to the student |
| Address | Home address and contact numbers |
| Health | Medical notes, blood type, emergency contact |
| Academic | Enrolled courses and assigned teachers per subject |
| Finance | Full payment history, outstanding balance, fee records |
| Attendance | Attendance records per date and per course, with summary stats |
| Performance | Marks and grades per subject and semester |

**Actions you can take:**
- **Edit** any information in the student's profile — click the edit icon next to a field.
- **Activate / Deactivate** the student account (requires re-entering your admin password).
- **Chat** with the student directly from the detail panel.

---

## Managing Parents

Go to **Parents** in the left sidebar.

**The parent list:**
- All parents are shown in a searchable list.

**Clicking on a parent shows:**
- Their full profile (name, email, phone, address).
- All children linked to their account, with grade, section, and the relationship (Mother, Father, Guardian, etc.).

**Actions:**
- Activate / deactivate the parent account.

---

## Class Schedule Builder

**Tasks:**

## Taking Attendance

Go to **Attendance** in the left sidebar.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Select the **course** you want to take attendance for from the dropdown list. Only your assigned courses appear.
2. Select the **date** using the date picker.
3. Your student list will load, showing each student with a Present / Absent toggle switch.
4. Toggle each student to mark them as **Present** (green) or **Absent** (red).
5. The system **saves automatically** — you do not need to click a Save button. Changes are saved 0.9 seconds after you stop toggling.

> **Tip:** If you prefer to save manually, go to **Settings** and turn off Auto-save for Attendance. In manual mode you must click **Save** yourself. While there are unsaved changes you cannot switch to a different course or date until you save or discard.

---

## Entering Student Marks (Grades)

Go to **Marks** in the left sidebar.

**The grading system uses three components that add up to 100:**

| Component | Maximum Score | Description |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Mark 20 | 20 | First continuous assessment |
| Mark 30 | 30 | Second continuous assessment |
| Mark 50 | 50 | Final exam score |
| **Total** | **100** | Calculated automatically |

**Step-by-step:**
1. Select the **course** from the dropdown.
2. All students in that course are listed with three input boxes each (for mark20, mark30, mark50).
3. Enter the score for each student in each box.
4. The system saves automatically (0.9-second debounce, same as attendance).

---

## Writing Lesson Plans

Go to **Lesson Plan** in the left sidebar.

The Lesson Plan section has two views: **Annual Plan** and **Weekly Plan**.

### Annual Plan

This is your full-year curriculum overview. Each row represents one week of the school year.

**Fields to fill in for each week:**

| Field | What to write |
|-------|--------------|
| Month | The Ethiopian calendar month (e.g., Meskerem, Tekemt...) |
| Week | Week number in that month (W1, W2...) |
| Objective | The learning goal for that week |
| Topic | The chapter or subject matter being taught |
| Method | How you will teach it (lecture, group work, etc.) |
| Material | Resources you will use (textbook, charts, etc.) |
| Assessment | How you will assess students (quiz, oral test, etc.) |
| Expected Days | How many teaching days you plan for this week |

As you submit daily lesson logs (see below), the system automatically calculates your **Progress %** and shows whether you are Ahead, On Track, Behind, or Not Started.

### Weekly Plan

Switch to the **Weekly** tab to enter a detailed daily breakdown.

For each teaching day, fill in:
- **Date** — the actual date
- **Content** — what you taught
- **Objectives** — specific learning objectives for that day
- **Materials** — resources used
- **Activities** — classroom activities done
- **Assessment** — how you assessed learning that day

Once a day's lesson is done, click **Submit** to mark it as completed. This updates your Annual Plan progress automatically.

**Auto-save:** Both Annual and Weekly plans save automatically as you type (0.9-second delay).

**Export your lesson plan:**
- Click **Export PDF** to download a professionally formatted PDF.
- Click **Export Excel** to download a spreadsheet with all your plan data.

---

## Creating Exams

Go to **Exam** in the left sidebar.

Here you build a question bank organized by the school curriculum.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Select the **subject** and **grade**.
2. Select the **chapter** from the curriculum list.
3. Click **Add Question**.
4. Write your question and fill in options **A, B, C, D**.
5. Select the **correct answer**.
6. Set the point value and optionally add an explanation.
7. Choose the **assessment type**: Exam, Quiz, or Assignment.
8. Set the duration and pass score.
9. Click **Publish** when the exam is ready for students.

---

## Messaging (Chat)

Go to **All Chat** in the left sidebar.

**How it works:**
- Your list of conversations appears on the left — students, parents, and other teachers you have chatted with.
- Click any conversation to open it on the right.
- Type your message in the box at the bottom and press Enter (or click Send).
- You can also send images and videos.
- A small badge shows how many unread messages you have from each person.

**Quick Chat from the Students page:**
While browsing the Students page, click the **Chat** button on a student's detail panel to open a quick chat popup without leaving the page.

---

## Viewing Your Students

Go to **Students** in the left sidebar.

**What you see:**
- All students in your assigned courses, with their name, grade, section, and profile photo.
- Use the **grade/section filter** or **search bar** to find a specific student.

**Clicking a student opens a detail panel with three tabs:**

| Tab | What you see |
|-----|-------------|
| Details | Full profile: name, grade, gender, date of birth, student ID, linked parent names |
| Attendance | Attendance history for the last 45, 90, or 180 days. Shows percentage present/absent |
| Performance | Marks per subject, per semester. Shows score breakdown by component |

**Adding notes about a student:**
On the Details tab you can add, edit, or delete personal notes about a student. These notes are only visible to you.

---

## Timetable

Go to **Timetable** in the left sidebar.

This shows your personal weekly class schedule — which grade/section you teach, which subject, and at what time. This is set by the admin and is read-only for teachers.

---

## Settings

Go to **Settings** in the left sidebar.

**Things you can do:**

- Change your **password** — you must enter your current password first, then your new password (minimum 8 characters with letters and numbers).

## Year-End Rollover

> **Warning: This is the most important and sensitive operation in the system. Please read carefully before proceeding.**

The rollover archives the current academic year and resets the school for the next year. It promotes, repeats, or graduates students based on their marks.

**The rollover has a 3-step safety process:**

### Step 1 — Arm the Rollover
1. Go to **Academic Year** and click **Arm Rollover**.
2. Enter your **registerer password** to verify your identity.
3. Type the exact confirmation phrase shown on the screen:
   `ROLL OVER FROM 2024_2025 TO 2025_2026`
   (replace with the actual years shown)
4. Choose a **delay**: 1 hour, 6 hours, 12 hours, or 24 hours.
   This delay is mandatory — it gives time to cancel if you armed it by mistake.
5. Click **Arm**.

### Step 2 — Wait for the Countdown
A countdown timer will appear. The rollover cannot be executed until it reaches zero.

**To cancel:** Click **Cancel Rollover** at any time while the countdown is running.

### Step 3 — Execute the Rollover
Once the countdown reaches zero, the **Execute Rollover** button becomes available.

Click it to start the rollover. The system will:
- Archive all student records, marks, and lesson plans to the Year History.
- Reset attendance, chats, and other yearly data.
- Automatically promote students who passed (score ≥ 50% by default) to the next grade.
- Mark students who failed as repeating their current grade.
- Graduate students in the final grade.
- Deactivate all student and parent accounts until the new year begins.

> After the rollover, you will need to re-activate student accounts manually or re-register students for the new year.

## Terminated Employees

Go to **Terminated** in the left sidebar.

This shows all employees who have been terminated. Their records are archived but not deleted.

### How to Terminate an Employee

From the **Employees** page, find the employee and click **Terminate**.

A form will appear asking for:
- **Reason** (required) — why the employee is being terminated.
- **Last working date** — the final day of employment.
- **Note** — any additional notes.

Click **Confirm Termination**. The system will:
- Mark the employee's status as Terminated.
- Deactivate their login account.
- Archive their role record (Teacher, Finance, etc.).
- Create a termination record for the audit history.

> **Important for teachers:** If a teacher still has active academic assignments (courses, students), the system will warn you. You must deactivate them in the Admin or Registerer portal first before terminating them in HR.
